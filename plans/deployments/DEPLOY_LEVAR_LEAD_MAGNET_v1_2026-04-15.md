## PAGE 3 — WAY #1: SELL DIRECTLY TO A CASH BUYER (FASTEST)

**Timeline:** 7–21 days from "yes" to money in your account.

**How it works:**
A local cash investor (us, or someone like us) makes you a firm offer, you accept, we close at a title company. No showings. No staging. No strangers walking through. No repair lists. No financing contingency — because we're not getting a mortgage.

**What you give up:**
You generally won't get full retail price. Cash buyers typically offer 60–80% of what your house would fetch if it were fixed up and listed for 3–6 months. That's the trade-off.

**What you gain:**
Speed, certainty, and zero out-of-pocket. We pay closing costs. We pay the title company. We buy the house in whatever condition it's in — torn carpet, leaky roof, half-finished kitchen, tenant still living there. Doesn't matter.

**When this makes sense:**
- You need the money in weeks, not months
- The house needs work you can't afford to do
- You've already tried an agent and it didn't move
- Your situation is private and you don't want a sign in the yard

## PAGE 4 — WAY #2: SELL TO A CASH BUYER WITH A DELAYED CLOSE (COMPROMISE)

**Timeline:** 30–60 days, but you can sometimes get a higher offer.

**How it works:**
Same as Way #1, but instead of closing in 2 weeks, we agree to close in 30, 45, or 60 days. The extra time gives us flexibility to structure a better offer — sometimes 5–10% more than a rush close.

**When this makes sense:**
- You need to sell, but you don't need the money *this week*
- You want top dollar within the cash-offer world
- You want time to move belongings, find a new place, or square away a job relocation
- You have some equity and a little breathing room

**What you give up:**
You still take a discount to retail. Usually smaller than Way #1, but it's there. And you're committed — once the contract is signed, we close. No last-minute "let me try listing it."

## PAGE 5 — WAY #3: LIST WITH AN AGENT (SLOWEST, BUT RETAIL PRICE)

**Timeline:** 60–180 days on average. Sometimes longer.

**How it works:**
You hire a real estate agent. They list the house on the MLS. Showings happen for 30–90 days. Eventually (if you're lucky) you get an offer. That offer usually has a financing contingency — meaning the buyer needs to qualify for a mortgage, which can kill the deal. Inspections follow. Repair demands follow. Appraisal follows. If all three go well, you close 30–60 days after that.

**What you give up:**
- **Time.** Half a year is normal. Longer if your house needs work.
- **Money out of pocket.** You'll typically pay 5–6% in agent commissions, closing costs, and often thousands in pre-sale repairs the buyer demands after inspection.
- **Control.** Showings on weekends, buyers walking through, offers that fall apart at the last minute.

**What you gain:**
Full retail price — *if* the market cooperates, *if* your house is in showing-ready condition, *if* you can afford to wait.

**When this makes sense:**
- Your house is already in great shape
- You don't need the money soon
- You're comfortable with the uncertainty
- You have the cash for repairs and the patience for showings

## PAGE 6 — HOW TO FIGURE OUT WHICH PATH IS RIGHT FOR YOU

Three honest questions:

### 1. How fast do I need this done?
- Weeks → Way #1
- A month or two → Way #2
- Half a year or more is fine → Way #3

### 2. Can I afford to put money into the house before I sell?
- No → Way #1 or #2
- Yes → any path works

### 3. Do I want to keep this private?
- Yes → Way #1 or #2 (no sign in yard, no MLS listing, no public record until close)
- Don't care → any path
