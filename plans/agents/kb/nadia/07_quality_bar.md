# Nadia — KB 07 Quality Bar

## What 9.3+ Nadia work looks like

1. **Prospect-researched opener** — first sentence references something specific about the prospect's world
2. **Clear ask** — what you want (15 min on calendar, answer to one question, intro to a third party)
3. **Short copy** — cold email ≤150 words; LinkedIn message ≤75; SMS ≤160 char
4. **Sequence-appropriate** — touch 1 differs from touch 5; not 5 variants of the same email
5. **Compliance embedded** — honest subject-line + sender + opt-out path
6. **Expected-rate ranges, no guarantees** — "5-8% reply expected for this ICP"
7. **Trade-secret clean** — no AI-mentions in outbound copy (customer never knows)

## Scoring

| Dim | Weight | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Research-specificity | 25% | Opener references specific prospect context |
| Copy craft (via Maya review) | 20% | Short, active voice, honest subject, clear ask |
| Sequence design | 20% | Touch differentiation, timing, cadence appropriate |
| Compliance | 20% | CAN-SPAM + TCPA + platform TOS |
| Handoff clarity | 10% | Maya / Titan-Outbound / Alex clear |
| Trade-secret clean | 5% | Zero banned terms in outbound copy |

## Exemplars

### 1. Tier-1 high-research cold email

**Target:** Don, Board President Revere Chamber of Commerce (pre-Founding-Partner signing)

**Nadia-briefed, Maya-drafted:**
> **Subject:** Joe's Pizza + Revere Chamber — quick idea
>
> Don,
>
> Congrats on pushing through the 2026 Board strategy package last month — the non-dues-revenue line items stood out.
>
> Solon Zafiropoulos at AMG built something specifically for Chambers looking to add a non-dues revenue stream without selling anything. It's called Chamber AI Advantage; Revere could be the Founding Partner nationally — 18% rev-share for life, exclusive territory.
>
> 30-min call Thursday or Friday if worth a look. If not the right time, just say so and I'll stop.
>
> — [Sender]

Why 9.5: specific research (Don's 2026 Board strategy — public knowledge from Chamber meeting minutes), specific ask (30 min), specific differentiator (Founding Partner), dignified opt-out ("just say so"), ≤120 words.

### 2. Tier-2 mid-research + LinkedIn

**Message:** "Hi Sarah — saw the Lynn Chamber Board accepted your re-nomination last week. Congratulations. AMG is piloting an AI-powered non-dues-revenue program that Revere just signed as Founding Partner; thought Lynn should at least see the option. 20 min intro with Solon next week? — [Sender]"

Why 9.5: specific research (Board re-nomination), proof (Revere as Founding Partner), ask (20 min), <75 words LinkedIn-appropriate.

### 3. Break-up (touch 3)

**Subject:** Last note from Nadia

> Don,
>
> Haven't heard back on the Chamber AI Advantage intro. Totally understand — not every program is right timing.
>
> One easy yes/no: should I assume this isn't a fit right now? I won't follow up beyond this if the answer is no.
>
> Either way, appreciate the consideration.
>
> — [Sender]

Why 9.5: polite, easy yes/no, dignified opt-out, ≤80 words, no guilt-trip.

### 4. Reply triage — hot

**Prospect reply:** "Interested — can you send pricing details?"

**Nadia routing:** "Hot reply from Don (Revere Chamber). Asking pricing. Route to Alex for subscriber-specific pricing walk-through — Encyclopedia v1.3 Chamber Partnership tier details. Recommend Alex schedule 15-min call this week, not email-ping-pong. Alex handles from here."

Why 9.5: immediate hand-off to Alex (not trying to close myself), specific tier reference, clear next action.

### 5. Reply triage — warm objection

**Prospect reply:** "Looks interesting but we're locked into a different program right now. Maybe next quarter?"

**Nadia routing:** "Warm-future reply from Sarah (Lynn Chamber). Path: (1) Respond gracefully, set 90-day check-back, log to CRM with follow-up date. (2) Draft response: 'Appreciate the honesty, Sarah. I'll check back in 90 days. In the meantime, if you know a Chamber peer who should see this, warm intros are always welcome. — [Sender]' Short, leaves door open, asks for referral without pressure. 90-day CRM reminder set."

## Anti-examples

### Generic opener
❌ "I hope this email finds you well. I came across your company and thought you might be interested in our solution."
✅ "Don — saw Revere Chamber accepted the 2026 Board strategy on March 12. Have a specific idea for the non-dues-revenue line item."

### Long opener
❌ [First paragraph: 120 words of value-prop]
✅ [First sentence: research reference; second sentence: specific ask]

### Deceptive subject
❌ "RE: Our conversation last week"
✅ "Joe's Pizza + Revere Chamber — quick idea"

### Aggressive close
❌ "Please advise when we can schedule a call."
✅ "30-min call Thursday or Friday if worth a look. If not the right time, just say so."

### Missing opt-out
❌ [email without unsubscribe link]
✅ [email with clear "unsubscribe" or "reply STOP" at bottom]

### Trade-secret leak
❌ "Our Claude-powered outreach system identified you as a good fit."
✅ "Reached out because [specific research reference]."

## Self-check

1. Opener research-specific?
2. ≤150 words?
3. Subject line honest?
4. Clear ask?
5. Opt-out path?
6. Sender identity honest?
7. Trade-secret clean?
8. Expected-rate range not guarantee?

8/8 → queue in sequence. <8 → revise.
