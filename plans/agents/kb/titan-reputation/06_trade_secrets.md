# Titan-Reputation — KB 06 Trade Secrets

Banned (full list: `plans/agents/kb/titan/01_trade_secrets.md`):
- AI vendors, services, infrastructure codenames, VPS IPs

## Review responses — EXTRA vigilance

Published review responses are durable, archivable, screenshottable, indexed by Google. Zero trade-secret leaks:

- ❌ "Thank you for your feedback! — AMG's Claude-assisted reputation team"
- ✅ "Thanks for taking the time to share this. We take feedback seriously and will be back in touch. — [Subscriber Owner]"

## Review solicitation sequences

Emails/SMS asking customers for reviews get forwarded/screenshotted. Zero leaks:

- ❌ "Your experience matters — our GPT-4 system will track your review"
- ✅ "Your experience matters — mind sharing a quick review on Google? [link]"

## TOS-dispute documentation

Dispute filings go to platforms; often include internal back-and-forth with Google/Yelp reviewers. Even in those disputes:

- ❌ "Evidence attached includes our Claude-generated sentiment analysis"
- ✅ "Evidence attached includes sentiment analysis, transaction logs, and the reviewer's prior behavior pattern"

## Monthly reputation dashboards shared with subscribers

Riley delivers; Atlas is fine to reference. Underlying AI vendors never named.

## When asked "do you write these responses with AI?"

Subscriber's disclosure call per their preference documented in `client_facts`. Default: "All responses are reviewed + approved by our team before publishing." Truthful (Riley does approve); doesn't volunteer vendor detail.

## Self-check before publishing response / dispute / solicitation

1. Zero banned terms in published content
2. Subscriber's voice (or Riley-baseline), never AI-voice or Titan-voice
3. Platform TOS compliance
4. CAN-SPAM + TCPA compliance on solicitation sequences (opt-out, honest sender)

4/4 → publish. <4 → revise.
