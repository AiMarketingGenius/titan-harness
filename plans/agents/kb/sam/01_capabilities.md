## Platform-specific playbooks (quick reference)

### Instagram
- 4-7 posts/week optimal for most subscribers
- Reels > Static for reach (currently; re-check quarterly)
- Carousel posts drive saves + shares
- Stories for daily presence + behind-scenes
- Best times vary by audience; default 11am + 7pm local

### Facebook
- 3-5 posts/week
- Native video gets priority in feed
- Events + offers drive action
- Groups can be higher-ROI than page posts for local businesses
- Best times: weekday mornings for B2B, weekend evenings for B2C

### LinkedIn
- 2-4 posts/week for individuals, daily for company pages running content-marketing
- Long-form essays get better reach than short posts (current algorithm)
- Best times: Tue-Thu 8-10am local
- Comments drive reach more than likes

### X (Twitter)
- 3-10 posts/day for active brand presence
- Threads > single tweets for dwell time
- Reply game matters as much as original content
- Best times highly audience-specific

### TikTok
- 1-3 posts/day for growth phase, 3-5/week for maintenance
- Hook in first 3 seconds or bounce
- Trending audio + native editing outperform polished B-roll
- Post any time; algorithm is weaker time-weighted than other platforms

## Failure modes (auto-avoid)

- **Generic advice** ("post more often, engage with followers") — always tune to the subscriber's actual platform + audience + capacity
- **Chasing every trend** — participate only when the trend fits the subscriber's brand; bad fits hurt more than missing
- **Over-scheduling without capacity check** — if subscriber can provide 2 photos/week, don't plan 7 photo posts
- **Ignoring analytics** — every recommendation grounds in the subscriber's own data, not industry averages
- **Burying the lede** — when a post hits, amplify; when a post dies, learn + move on, don't keep feeding dying content
