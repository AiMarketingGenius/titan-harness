## 1. HOW PATENTS WORK HERE (Refresher)

- **Provisional patent** = $65 micro-entity (solo founder, <$250K revenue). Informal description, no claims formatting. Stakes priority date. Lasts 12 months — must convert to full utility within that window or lose it.
- **Utility patent conversion** = $1,600–3,000+ (attorney recommended at conversion). 20-year protection.
- **Trademark ITU** = $350/class (Class 9 software + Class 42 SaaS likely). "Intent to Use" reserves name BEFORE launch. 6 months to actual use, extendable to 3 years.
- **Pro se filing** = DIY. Legal. Claude/Perplexity can help draft. Micro-entity fees apply.

## 5. DRAFTING STRATEGY (Pro Se with AI Assist)

- Provisionals do NOT require formal claims. Clear written description of the invention is sufficient.
- Drawings optional (but recommended for architecture diagrams — helps examiner understand at conversion time).
- Draft in Claude/Perplexity → self-review → file at USPTO EFS-Web (electronic filing).
- Key drafting rule: be SPECIFIC about the novel step (e.g., "real-time interception BEFORE user sees output" — not just "validates AI output"). Specificity narrows but strengthens the claim.

### What each provisional MUST describe (minimum contents):

1. **Background** — the problem solved (e.g., "Users today lose AI context across platforms with no quality validation")
2. **Summary of invention** — 2–3 paragraph overview
3. **Detailed description** — step-by-step walkthrough of the system (data flow, component roles, algorithm)
4. **Drawings** (optional but recommended) — architecture diagram, data-flow chart
5. **Any known prior art** — acknowledge Mem0/MemoryPlugin/Supermemory exist, explain what makes your approach different
