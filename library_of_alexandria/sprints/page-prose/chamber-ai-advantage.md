## SECTION 7 — FAQ (alternate #0F172A, FAQPage schema)

**H2:** Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: How can a Chamber of Commerce use AI?**
Chambers use AI across four layers: internal operations (member CRM, events, email, reporting), member-benefit delivery (AI marketing at Chamber-member pricing), outbound and recruitment (AI-driven prospect identification and outreach), and governance plus reporting (Board-ready summaries and documented AI policy). The Chamber AI Advantage program packages all four into a single channel partnership.

**Q2: What's the best way for a Chamber to generate non-dues revenue?**
The fastest-growing non-dues revenue category in 2026 is channel partnerships — the Chamber delivers a vetted service to members and earns a margin on every sale. Traditional affinity programs (insurance, office supplies) are commoditized. AI marketing channel partnerships are uncommoditized, high-value, recurring, and aligned with what member businesses actually need to grow.

**Q3: What does a Chamber channel partner program look like?**
A Chamber channel partner program is a revenue-sharing agreement where the Chamber offers a vetted service to members under Chamber branding and earns a percentage of every subscription. The Chamber AI Advantage program is structured as a Founding Partner agreement: 18% lifetime rev-share on member subscriptions for Founding Chambers (15% standard), white-labeled delivery, Chamber-controlled branding, zero platform cost for the member-benefit layer.

**Q4: Should a Chamber invest in AI tools?**
Yes — but with governance. Chambers that adopt AI without a Board-approved policy take on unnecessary risk. The Chamber AI Advantage program includes a Board-ready AI Policy Template, documented data handling, and regular compliance updates. Governance is how Chambers move faster without creating exposure.

**Q5: How is this different from ChamberMaster or GrowthZone?**
Traditional Chamber management software (ChamberMaster, GrowthZone, MemberClicks) is built for internal Chamber operations — roster management, event registration, billing. The Chamber AI Advantage program is built for member revenue — delivering AI marketing to member businesses as a Chamber-branded benefit, with the Chamber earning a margin on every subscription. The two are complementary, not competitive. Chambers can run both.

**Q6: What does Founding Partner status mean?**
Founding Partners are the first 10 Chambers to adopt the program globally. Founding Partners earn 18% lifetime rev-share (vs. 15% standard), lock setup pricing at Founding terms, receive priority onboarding, and get reference-case positioning. Once all 10 slots are claimed, standard terms apply for every subsequent Chamber.

**Q7: What's the delivery timeline?**
Layer 1 (Chamber website rebuild + AI support) is delivered in 3–4 weeks from deposit. Layer 2 (member platform) launches immediately after Layer 1 ships, typically Week 5–6. Layer 3 (Chamber OS) is modular and can begin any time — most Chambers start Month 3+, funded by Layer 2 rev-share that's already coming in.

**Q8: What's the risk to our Chamber if member adoption is soft?**
Layer 2 (the member-benefit program) has zero platform cost to the Chamber. Layer 1 (website rebuild) is a one-time investment with value regardless of Layer 2 adoption. The launch webinar, co-op marketing match, and Board-ready member communications template are all included to accelerate adoption. If a Chamber purchases Exclusive Territory, quota-miss rules include a 30-day cure period, and the Chamber retains all existing member relationships and rev-share on those.

**Q9: How is this different from the 20 vendors pitching my Chamber every quarter?**
Traditional Chamber vendors sell the Chamber a tool the Chamber pays for. The Chamber AI Advantage program inverts that — AMG pays the Chamber a share of every member subscription sold through the channel. The Chamber's budget isn't a cost center for this program; it's a revenue line. Regional exclusivity means the first Chamber in a market closes the door behind them — vendors pitch every Chamber in every region, but Founding Partners get contractual first-mover protection.
