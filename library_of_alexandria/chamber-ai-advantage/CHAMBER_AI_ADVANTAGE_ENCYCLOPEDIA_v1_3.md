## 2. THE THREE-LAYER PRODUCT ARCHITECTURE

Every Chamber engagement has three distinct layers. These are separately priced, separately contracted, and can be adopted sequentially.

## 3. THE CHANNEL-ONLY DISTRIBUTION MODEL

**AMG does not sell to businesses directly.** Ever. (Exception: custom AI systems buildouts for companies — see Section 19.)

**This is not a preference. It is a contractual commitment.** AMG's business model is built on the channel being our entire go-to-market for member-business products. Every Chamber that signs becomes a protected sales territory for its product suite. The channel-first commitment is non-negotiable, because Chamber partnerships only work if partners trust that AMG won't circumvent them.

### 3.0 Why This Matters — Lessons From Oracle

Solon spent years at Oracle, where the VAR (Value-Added Reseller) channel was the lifeblood of the business. The company made recurring mistakes — going direct to accounts that belonged to partners, undercutting partner pricing, competing against its own resellers. Every time, it damaged the channel's trust and took years to rebuild.

**AMG does not repeat those mistakes.** Channel-first is written into our operating DNA:
- No direct sales of Chamber AI Advantage subscription products
- No direct sales of AI Memory Vault bulk licenses (only through Chambers)
- No pricing games — published Chamber member rates are the rates
- No "special deals" to Chamber member businesses without the Chamber earning rev-share
- Co-op marketing dollars MATCHED by AMG to every Chamber's earnings (Section 23)
- Channel Managers assigned as relationship owners (Section 24)

### 3.1 Contractual Commitment

Every Chamber Partnership Agreement contains a clause obligating AMG to route all inbound individual business leads through its local Chamber partner network:

> *"Provider agrees that it will not directly sell the [Chamber AI Advantage] subscription product to individual businesses in territories covered by a signed Chamber partner. All inbound prospects will be routed to their nearest participating Chamber of Commerce partner for enrollment."*

### 3.2 The AMG Website → Chamber Funnel

aimarketinggenius.io stays live at full retail pricing ($497/$797/$1,497). No public self-signup remains. Instead:

1. Prospect lands on aimarketinggenius.io, interested in subscription
2. AI chatbot (on AMG's site) asks: *"Where's your business located?"*
3. System identifies prospect's metro area, looks up participating Chamber
4. Chatbot presents offer:
   > *"Great news — businesses in [City] get exclusive access to our platform through [Chamber Name]. Your Chamber member price is $[discounted rate]/month instead of $[retail]. You'll need to be an active Chamber member to enroll. Here's your Chamber contact — mention AMG AI Advantage when you apply for membership."*
5. Prospect sent to Chamber with referral tag
6. Chamber receives warm lead + recurring rev-share on conversion

### 3.3 Territories Without a Chamber Partner

If a prospect is in a territory without an active Chamber partner:
- **Option A (preferred):** Route to nearest Chamber partner within 50 miles
- **Option B (if Option A fails):** Add them to a waitlist + notify them when their local Chamber joins

**There is NO Option C.** AMG does not sell subscription products directly to businesses. Ever. If no Chamber covers a prospect's territory, they wait. This preserves the exclusivity that makes Chambers want to partner in the first place.

**Exception:** Custom AI systems buildouts for enterprise clients ($25K–$250K+ engagements) are separately handled via direct AMG consulting — these are NOT the subscription product and do not compete with the Chamber channel (see Section 19).

### 3.4 Resellers Reselling Baby Atlas

**Chambers that reach 50+ active member subscriptions unlock an advanced right:**
- They may resell custom Baby Atlas / Chamber OS deployments to their own member businesses
- 20% margin to the Chamber on each Baby Atlas deal
- Custom Baby Atlas pricing: $2,500 setup + $397/mo base per deployment
- AMG retains fulfillment responsibility; Chamber is the sales + relationship holder

This transforms high-performing Chambers into revenue franchises.

## 6. EXCLUSIVE TERRITORY (PREMIUM ADD-ON)

### 6.1 What It Is

By default, Chamber partnerships are **non-exclusive**. Multiple Chambers can participate in overlapping regions. However, any Chamber may purchase exclusive territory rights.

### 6.2 Exclusivity Pricing

| Chamber Tier | Territory Exclusivity Fee (Annual) |
|--------------|-------------------------------------|
| Small Chamber | Not offered (only non-exclusive) |
| Mid-Size Chamber | $12,000/year |

### 6.3 Territory Definition

- **Default:** 20-mile radius from Chamber HQ OR Chamber's stated service area (whichever is larger)
- **Metro exception:** For large metros (Boston, NYC, Chicago, LA), territory = full metro statistical area

### 6.4 Performance Quotas to Maintain Exclusivity

| Chamber Tier | Quota to Maintain Exclusive Territory |

| Mid-Size | 25 active member subscriptions OR $12K MRR within 6 months |
| Large Metro | 50 active member subscriptions OR $25K MRR within 6 months |

If quota missed: 30-day cure period. If still missed: territory reopens to non-exclusive status; other Chambers may enter. Original Chamber keeps all existing member relationships and rev-share on those.

## 7. REVENUE SHARE STRUCTURE

### 7.1 Standard Rev-Share Terms

- **15% of net collected subscription revenue** from Chamber-referred members
- Paid monthly, 15 days after close of each calendar month
- "Net collected" = payments received, minus taxes, refunds, chargebacks, processing reversals
- ACH payout to Chamber bank account
- Monthly statement showing active accounts, collected revenue, commission due

### 7.2 Founding Rev-Share

First 10 Chambers: **18%** instead of 15%, locked for life.

### 7.3 Bonus Milestones (Chamber-Level, Not Individual)

Paid to Chamber treasury, NOT to individuals. Triggered by active subscription thresholds:

- No new signups under the Chamber's referral tag after termination date

This protects the Chamber's earned revenue while closing the acquisition channel.

## 8. THE SPIFF PROGRAM (LEGAL, GIFT-CARD ONLY)

### 8.1 Why This Structure

Direct commissions to Chamber board members, executive directors, or other insiders trigger 501(c)(6) private inurement concerns. **We do not pay recurring commissions to individuals.** We pay the Chamber entity.

However, motivation matters. Chambers want to incentivize their staff and ambassadors to push member signups. The SPIFF program solves this legally.

### 8.2 SPIFF Tiers (Gift Cards, Not Cash)

Non-cash rewards for top-performing Chamber ambassadors:

| Performance Milestone | SPIFF Award |
|-----------------------|-------------|
| 1st active member subscription | $50 Dunkin' or Starbucks gift card |
| 3 active member subscriptions | $150 Amazon gift card |
| 5 active member subscriptions | $250 Amex gift card OR branded swag package |
| 10 active member subscriptions | $500 Amex gift card + restaurant dinner voucher |
| Top Ambassador of the Month | Custom plaque + $300 gift card + social media recognition |

### 8.3 Governance Requirements

Every SPIFF payout requires:
- Chamber's written approval (one-time blanket approval in the partnership agreement works)
- Compliance with Chamber's own conflict-of-interest policy
- Non-voting-board-member preference (voting Board members should opt out to avoid appearance of conflict)
- Annual disclosure on Chamber's 990 if applicable

### 8.4 What SPIFF Is NOT

- NOT recurring monthly commissions
- NOT a percentage of member MRR
- NOT cash payments
- NOT payments to voting Board members without explicit written Chamber policy approval

## 9. IMPLEMENTATION — THE 4-WEEK DELIVERY PLAYBOOK

Every Chamber delivery follows this playbook. Timeline: 3-4 weeks from signed contract + setup deposit received.

### Week 1 — Discovery + Harvest

**Deliverables:**
- Kickoff call with Chamber leadership (Board President + Executive Director + Marketing lead)
- Content harvest: emails, Board meeting minutes (last 24 months), event history, sponsor lists, member directory
- Technical audit: current website, CRM, email system, payment processor
- Stakeholder interviews (30 min each: Board President, Exec Director, Marketing contact)
- Branding asset collection: Chamber logo, color palette, approved typefaces, voice/tone guide
- Security baseline assessment
- **Chamber AI Implementation Spec** signed off by Chamber before Week 2 begins

### Week 2 — Build + Integration

**Deliverables:**
- Chamber-branded portal spun up with tenant_config + theme + agent aliases applied
- Baby Atlas harvest completes — Chamber OS knowledge base populated
- Website migration executed (Wix/Squarespace → WordPress)
- AI chatbot trained on Chamber-specific policies, FAQs, event info, member benefits
- CRM migration: existing member list imported, segmented by tier + industry
- Security hardening applied: Cloudflare WAF, tenant RLS, off-site backup, secrets management
- Integration with Chamber's existing tools (email provider, payment processor, calendar)

### Week 3 — Customization + Testing

**Deliverables:**
- Custom modules built (if contracted)
- Mobile Command configured for Board President
- Meeting Intelligence tested (Zoom integration verified)
- Member enrollment flow tested end-to-end (5 test signups minimum)
- AI agent responses tuned to Chamber voice (15 sample interactions reviewed with Chamber marketing lead)
- Load test: 50 concurrent users simulated
- Security penetration test: internal pen test, plus optional external firm test for Metro tier
- Staff training session (2 hours, Chamber team)
- User Acceptance Testing with 3-5 Chamber Board volunteers

### Week 4 — Launch + Webinar

**Deliverables:**
- Soft launch to Board members (3 days internal testing)
- Board sign-off meeting
- Public launch email campaign to Chamber membership
- Solon-hosted live member webinar (60 min + 30 min Q&A)
- Recorded webinar delivered to Chamber (evergreen asset)
- First 30-day success metrics dashboard handed to Chamber leadership
- Ongoing support channel established (dedicated Slack channel or email alias)

### 9.1 What Can Slow This Down

**Common blockers** (documented so we flag early):
- Chamber content not centralized (harvest takes extra week)
- Website migration blocked by missing legacy credentials
- Chamber staff unavailable for Week 1 interviews (delays spec sign-off)
- Custom module scope expansion mid-build
- Branding approval delays

All blockers tracked; any 48-hour slip triggers Solon notification.

**Co-op marketing** is a channel-partner benefit where AMG matches a percentage of each Chamber's partnership revenue as advertising dollars, deployed specifically to promote THAT Chamber's branded products (Revere AI Advantage, Boston AI Advantage, etc.).

Modeled on Oracle / Microsoft / Cisco VAR co-op programs that Solon experienced firsthand at Oracle.

### 23.2 Co-Op Match Structure

**AMG matches 1% of each Chamber's gross rev-share earnings as co-op advertising budget.**

Example:
- Chamber earns $10,000 in rev-share over Year 1
- AMG allocates $100 to co-op ad spend for that Chamber
- Ads run Facebook/Instagram/Google targeted to Chamber's geographic area
- All ad traffic routes to Chamber's enrollment page (NOT AMG's)

**Scaling benefit:** As Chamber earns more, co-op pool grows, more ads drive more member signups, which drives more earnings. Compounding loop.

### 23.3 Enhanced Co-Op for Top Performers

Chambers hitting certain thresholds get **increased match rates**:

| Performance Tier | Co-Op Match Rate | Qualification |
|------------------|------------------|---------------|
| Standard | 1% | All Chamber partners |
| Silver | 2% | 25+ active subscriptions OR $25K annual rev-share |
| Gold | 3% | 50+ active subscriptions OR $75K annual rev-share |
| Platinum | 5% | 100+ active subscriptions OR $150K annual rev-share |

Platinum Chambers get 5× the co-op match rate of Standard tier — a substantial acceleration of Chamber-branded advertising reach.

### 23.4 Ad Creative & Content Library

Chambers choose from pre-approved creative in the AMG Channel Marketing Library:
- Facebook carousel ads (customizable with Chamber logo/city)
- Instagram story templates
- Google Ads copy (editable geo-targeting)
- LinkedIn sponsored content
- Video ads (15, 30, 60 second cuts)
- Email marketing templates

All creative pre-tested and compliance-approved. Chambers can customize copy within guidelines.

**Custom creative:** $300-500/hr via AMG design team, complexity-tiered (see Section 26).

### 23.5 Campaign Types Pre-Built

- **Member Acquisition** — "Join [Chamber] to unlock exclusive AI marketing"
- **AI Memory Vault Launch** — "Your Chamber membership now includes..."
- **Retention** — "Here's why [Chamber] members are staying longer"
- **Event Promotion** — Chamber events promoted to local audiences
- **Founding Partner Announcement** — One-time press push for new launches

### 23.6 Co-Op Statement

Monthly statement delivered to every Chamber:
- Current co-op pool balance
- Year-to-date spend
- Campaign performance (impressions, clicks, signups attributed)
- 12-month rolling window (unused dollars don't expire mid-year)
- Upcoming recommended campaigns

### 23.7 Launch Support Beyond Co-Op

Every new Chamber partnership also receives (one-time, not from co-op pool):
- Launch announcement press release written + distributed
- Member email announcement template (Chamber-branded)
- "Member Welcome" campaign setup in Chamber's email system
- 90-day launch ad blitz (one-time $500-$2000 spend by AMG, Chamber-directed)

### 23.8 Transparency & Audit

All co-op spend is auditable. Chambers can request full receipt records any time. Unused co-op funds at Chamber termination are forfeited (do not transfer as cash). This protects AMG from Chambers stockpiling co-op credits and then cashing out.

## 25. PRICING TRANSPARENCY DOCTRINE & PRICE-LOCK POLICY

### 25.1 Public + Transparent Pricing (Non-Negotiable)

AMG's pricing is **public and transparent**. Every Chamber sees the same rate card. Every Chamber-member business sees the same subscription prices. There are no handshake deals, no hidden discounts, no whisper-network pricing.

**Why:**
- Channel trust requires predictability. Partners need to know they're not being undercut by the next Chamber signing.
- Transparency becomes a competitive moat against opaque agencies.
- Board members (our customers) respect predictable, defensible pricing.
- Eliminates negotiation drag — Chambers know the rate, sign or don't.

**What's published:**
- Setup fees by Chamber size ($4,997 / $9,997 / $19,997)
- Module pricing (Section 4.3)
- Subscription pricing ($497 / $797 / $1,497)
- Chamber member discount (15% off)
- Rev-share (15% standard, 18% founding)
- Bulk license rates for AI Memory Vault ($60 / $48 / $36 per-year per-member)
- Tiered hourly rates ($300 simple / $400 standard / $500 complex), Platinum volume discount — see Section 26

**What's NOT negotiated:**
- Subscription prices
- Rev-share percentages
- SPIFF gift card amounts
- Co-op match percentages

**What's flexible:**
- Custom hourly engagements (scoped per SOW)
- Exclusive territory fees (negotiable by market size)
- Founding Partner perks (closed to first 10 Chambers only)
- Volume hourly discounts for Platinum-tier Chambers (Section 26)

### 25.2 Price-Locked-For-Life Policy

**CORE COMMITMENT:** Once a Chamber signs at a given rate, those rates are locked **for the life of the active subscription**. Not month-to-month. Not year-to-year. Lifetime lock.

**This applies to:**
- Setup fees (already one-time, obviously locked)
- Chamber AI Advantage monthly subscription tiers for that Chamber's enrolled members
- Chamber's rev-share percentage
- AI Memory Vault bulk license rate
- Co-op match percentage (unless tier upgrade triggers higher match)

**Exception for individual member subscribers:** If a member subscribes at $677 (Growth tier, 15% off $797 retail) and cancels, they cannot return at $677 after future price increases. They re-subscribe at the then-current rate. Price lock is ACTIVE subscription only — canceling resets.

**Why this works:**
- Incentivizes early signers (locked pricing = a real asset)
- Prevents Chambers/members from churning and re-signing to game the system
- Matches how serious enterprise software contracts work (Oracle, Salesforce, etc.)
- Gives sales a real urgency lever: "Sign now, lock this rate forever."

### 25.3 Price Escalation Over Time

As adoption grows, published prices INCREASE on new Chambers and new subscribers:

| Milestone | Price Adjustment |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Launch through Founding 10 Chambers | Launch pricing (locked for founders forever) |
| Chambers 11-25 | Standard pricing (metro setup $29,997, small $5,997, mid $10,997) |
| Chambers 26-50 | +10% across all subscription tiers |
| Chambers 51-100 | +20% across all subscription tiers |
| Chambers 100+ | +30% across all subscription tiers |

**Example of what this means in practice:**
- Revere signs now at Founding #1 with Starter members at $497/$422. Those members subscribed before Chamber 11 pay $422 for life (15% off $497).
- If Boston signs at Chamber #22, their members pay $422 too (still pre-+10% threshold).
- If NYC signs at Chamber #35, their members pay $465 (15% off $547 new retail) — different rate than Boston.
- But Revere's existing members keep $422 forever because they're price-locked.

This creates clear urgency at every pricing threshold. Chambers and members racing to sign before the next price increase.

### 25.4 Non-Negotiable Exceptions

Only the following people can approve rate exceptions:
- Solon (as CEO)
- Future VP of Channel (written policy, not blanket authority)
- Solon in writing for "big player" deals only — defined as 5,000+ member chambers OR state/federal-level associations

All exceptions must be:
- Documented in writing with rationale
- Logged in the Chamber partnership record
- Reported to Channel Advisory Board quarterly (for transparency to other partners)

## 26. CUSTOMIZATION HOURLY RATES

### 26.1 Tiered Standard Rates

Custom work is priced by project complexity — NOT by how much the Chamber can pay. Public rate card:

| Complexity | Hourly Rate |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Simple** | $300/hour |
| **Standard** | $400/hour |
| **Complex** | $500/hour |

**What determines complexity:**

- **Simple ($300/hr):** Content edits, minor template adjustments, light data migration, straightforward customization within existing modules, copywriting, simple design tweaks
- **Standard ($400/hr):** Single-system integrations (CRM, email, calendar), workflow automation, custom reporting dashboards, branded module customization, moderate migration work
- **Complex ($500/hr):** Multi-system integrations, merchant processor integration (Paddle), custom AI agent training, bespoke architecture, security/compliance engineering, custom module development, legacy platform migration from unusual systems

Complexity tier is assessed during SOW scoping and documented in writing before work begins. No rate changes mid-engagement without mutual written agreement.

### 26.2 Volume Hourly Discounts (Platinum-Tier Only)

Top-tier Chambers get modest discounts on all complexity tiers:

| Chamber Tier | Simple | Standard | Complex |
|--------------|--------|----------|---------|
| Registered / Silver | $300/hr | $400/hr | $500/hr |
| Gold (5% discount) | $285/hr | $380/hr | $475/hr |
| Platinum (10% discount) | $270/hr | $360/hr | $450/hr |

Discount caps at 10% — protects margin on our highest-cost delivery resources.

### 26.3 Engagement Minimums

- Minimum engagement: 10 hours
- Statement of Work (SOW) required before any work begins
- 50% deposit up front
- 50% on delivery acceptance
- Hours tracked in 15-minute increments
- Monthly capacity caps per Chamber (avoid over-committing delivery team)

### 26.4 Common Custom Engagements

- **CRM Integration** (existing Salesforce, HubSpot, etc.): 15-40 hours, Standard tier
- **Paddle Payment Integration**: 10-25 hours, Complex tier
- **Legacy System Migration** (custom from unusual platforms): 20-60 hours, tier varies by platform
- **Bespoke Reporting Dashboard**: 10-30 hours, Standard tier
- **Custom AI Voice Training** (Chamber-specific vocabulary): 15-25 hours, Complex tier
- **Multi-System Integration Bundles**: scoped case-by-case, typically Complex tier

Every engagement goes through written SOW. No verbal quotes. No scope creep without written change orders.

### 26.5 "Don't Give Away the Farm" Rule

Platinum-tier Chambers already receive massive benefits (5% co-op match, Advisory Board seat, priority product access). The 10% hourly discount is intentionally capped. We never go below 10% on hourly rates regardless of Chamber size or relationship depth.

Reason: delivery team capacity is our hard constraint. Custom hours are high-margin but finite. Discounting further erodes margin without winning us anything we don't already have from these partners.

### 26.6 Payment Processing Note

AMG processes all payments via **Paddle** (primary merchant processor). Stripe integrations are not supported — this is an architectural decision based on Paddle's merchant-of-record model which handles international taxes and compliance.

## 27. GLOBAL EXPANSION VISION

### 27.1 Current Scope: United States Chambers of Commerce

v1.x of Chamber AI Advantage is focused exclusively on **U.S. Chambers of Commerce**. Reasons:
- Regulatory consistency (501(c)(6) framework, state-level variations manageable)
- Solon's network + relationships concentrated here
- Revere as flagship within driving distance
- Contract law + tax treatment understood

### 27.2 Phase 2: Global Chamber Network (Month 12+)

After U.S. momentum is proven (~25+ U.S. Chambers), expand internationally:

**Priority international markets:**
- Canada (similar Chamber model, English-language, NAFTA-adjacent)
- United Kingdom (British Chambers of Commerce — national network)
- Australia (Australian Chamber of Commerce and Industry)
- EU member states (country-specific Chambers exist in every major nation)
- Mexico (CANACO network)

**What changes for international Chambers:**
- Currency conversion (pricing denominated in USD, invoiced in local currency)
- GDPR / regional data protection compliance
- Local language support (AI Memory Vault + Chamber AI Advantage UI localization)
- Local payment processors (not just Paddle)
- Time-zone appropriate support
- Regional partner manager hires

**What stays the same:**
- Three-layer architecture
- Rev-share structure
- Channel-only distribution commitment
- Price-locked-for-life policy
- Founding Partner program (reset for each major region — "first 10 UK Chambers," "first 10 EU Chambers")

### 27.3 Phase 3: Trade Associations (Month 18+)

Beyond Chambers of Commerce, trade associations are a natural adjacent channel:

**Examples:**
- National Restaurant Association + state-level affiliates
- National Association of Home Builders
- American Medical Association (specific sub-specialties)
- State-level CPA societies
- Bar associations (legal services)
- Retail Federations
- Manufacturing associations

**Why this works:**
- Same 501(c)(6) legal structure as Chambers
- Same member-affinity-program precedent
- Members are business owners in specific verticals
- AI marketing value is vertical-specific (enabling deeper customization)
- Rev-share model translates directly

**Product variation:**
- "[Association Name] AI Advantage"
- Vertical-specific agent training (restaurants, builders, dentists, etc.)
- Industry-specific content libraries
- Compliance-aware for regulated industries (medical, legal, financial)

### 27.4 Product Extension Opportunities (Future)

Long-term possibilities once Chamber + Trade Association expansion is mature:
- **State-Level Government Contracts** (economic development offices)
- **University Alumni Associations** (professional development for members)
- **Non-Profit Federations** (sector-specific AI tools)
- **International Development Organizations** (capacity building for emerging markets)

All follow the same playbook: find a membership-based organization that already has a member-benefit program, propose AMG as a premium benefit with rev-share back to the organization.

### 27.5 Governance of Expansion

Before expanding to ANY new market or channel:
- Legal review (local compliance, contract adaptation)
- Product readiness audit (localization, currency, compliance)
- Financial model (unit economics for that market)
- Delivery capacity (can we actually onboard + support Chambers in that time zone?)
- Sales strategy (Solon direct? Partner sales? In-country rep?)

Expansion plan documented in its own sub-encyclopedia when the time comes. Not a pivot today. A north star.
